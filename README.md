## Guilds
Grand Grove - Organizes Guild efforts + Votes to create new Guilds as needed  
Guild - Organizes worker efforts, posts bounties  
Hunters - hunt bounties, propose Guild or Order policy changes  

Guild delegates are elected to do a specific task.
- if the task is being done not, or done shittily, delegate removed and replaced, penalized pending an investigation. That shit should not be taken lightly.
- Delegate is also replaced after a defined time period and cannot hold the same position two cycles in a row.

I was hoping the Guild only had to exist inside the bounty process and as delegates.

When hunters complete bounties posted by a guild, they earn voting tokens (which will expire after a set time)

Hunters can "belong" to whatever Guild they desire (as long as they aren't barred from serving that Guild) by holding voting points.

### Druids
I wanted to have a way for there to be no ruling class.  
I couldn't imagine one up or find one that I liked.  
The best archetype I can liken my ideal druid to is Gandalf the Grey. Achieving his goal through setting up those around him for success.  
Sen TenZ once said, "a good player can play good themselves, but a great player can make people around them play good"  

This is my attempt at making the ruling class live at the level of their poorest constituents, so that in order to raise their own standard of living, they must be able to do it for those they represent as well.  
Every time a coin is minted, 20% of it is charged to the ruling class (druids) as debt.  
- Entirely possible that it becomes cosmetic and Druids are permanently only allowed to access USW.

If your account is negative, you can't buy shit.  
You can only obtain what Universal Standard Welfare allows you to.  
Behold, the commons are the rulers.  
The cost of policy power.  
Skin in the game.  

Druids can:
- submit proposals for Guild bounties which require a Guild Delegate Approval vote
- submit proposals to add or remove delegate positions which require a Guild Approval vote
- submit proposals for constitutional policy changes which require a Grand Grove Approval vote
#### Renouncing the Cloak
If a wo/man of the cloak decides they no longer wish to be, all they have to do is resign. However, the debt incurred through minting will still impact their wallet.

Resignation from any Druid position (Delegate, Grand Grove, Druid) carries a service ban of 10 years, where you are not allowed to serve as a Druid in any capacity pending investigation.

This shit is tough, we get it, but you made a commitment, a sacrifice, and mfer you can't make those lightly.

# The Bounty System
Grand Grove decides on a course of action, works with Guilds to build the plan, the guilds work with grand grove to develop bounty campaigns, the hunters work on the campaigns.

Bounties have two rewards, the first is a payment in credits the second is voting power (policy points for electing delegates and voting on guild/order policy changes), with the exception of Druid bounties, which have no reward other than society lives on.
## Credits
 20% of minted credits becomes Order Debt charged across all serving.
 - 10 credits minted, 2 druids serving, each charged one credit.
 - Druid's cannot redeem credits if they don't have any, and will thusly have to rely on USW
 - ok maybe it spreads 6-3-1, 6 Grand Grove members, 3 Delegates, 1 Druids.
	 - 10 credits minted, .12 charged to the single Grand Grove member, .6 to the single Delegate, and .2 to the single druid

There is no peer-peer credit trading.  
Credits can ONLY be spent on bounties, in-network vendors, or traded for their weight in Order profits in $USD.

We will be reinforcing against attempts at peer-peer trading.
